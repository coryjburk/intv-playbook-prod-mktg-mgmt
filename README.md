## 1. Welcome

The **PMM Interview Playbook** is a practice tool built by Eccles MBA Career Services to help you prepare for Product Marketing Manager interviews — along with adjacent GTM Strategy, Product Adoption, and Growth Marketing roles.

It gives you **100 real interview questions** across the eight areas a PMM panel will test, model answers and coaching for each, and a private practice room where you can say your answer out loud, get it scored, and watch your readiness build over time.

You don't need to install anything or create an account. It runs in your web browser, and your progress saves automatically as you go.

> [!TIP]
> Treat this like a gym, not a textbook. The biggest gains come from practicing answers out loud and getting them evaluated — not just reading the model answers.

When it loads, you'll see a navigation menu (down the left side on a computer, across the top on a phone) with five areas:

## 3. The Question Bank

This is the heart of the Playbook. The 100 questions are spread across eight areas (Positioning, Go-to-Market, Customer Insights, Growth, Competitive Intelligence, Stakeholder Alignment, Pricing, and Behavioral) and three difficulty levels (**Foundational**, **Core**, and **Advanced**).

### Finding the right questions

1. **Filter** — use the *Filter by domain* checkboxes to focus on one or more areas you want to work on.
2. **Difficulty** — use the *Difficulty* buttons to narrow to Foundational, Core, or Advanced.
3. **Search** — use the search box to find questions by keyword (for example, "pricing" or "churn").

### Reading a question

Click any question to expand it. Inside you'll find up to three tabs:

- **Model Answer** — how a strong candidate would answer.
- **Coaching** — a short insight on what the interviewer is really looking for and how to stand out.
- **Red Flag** — the specific way this kind of answer goes wrong, so you can avoid it.

### Two kinds of Model Answer

Foundational and behavioral questions tend to have one recognizable "gold-standard" answer, so those show a **full written model answer** you can study.

The open-ended scenario questions ("design the launch for…", "how would you respond to…") don't have a single right answer, so instead of one script to memorize they give you:

- **What this one tests** — what the interviewer is really probing with this question.
- **How to approach it** — the specific moves to make for this exact scenario.

On those questions you'll also see a **Generate a full model answer** button. Use it when you want to see a complete worked example to compare your thinking against.

> [!TIP]
> The scenario questions are meant to make you think, not recite. Read *What this one tests* and *How to approach it*, build your own answer first, then generate the full example to check yourself.

## 4. Practicing an answer

Reading is useful, but interviews are spoken. The Practice room lets you rehearse a real, out-loud answer and get it measured.

1. **Open the room** — open a question and click **Practice this**. A practice window opens with the question at the top.
2. **Record or type** — click **Start recording** and answer out loud. Your words appear in the answer box as you speak. (Prefer to type? You can type directly into the box instead.)
3. **Watch your delivery** — as you talk, the tool tracks your **Duration**, your **Pace** in words per minute, and your **Filler words** ("um," "like," "you know").
4. **Finish** — click **Stop recording** when you're done, and review what you said.
5. **Evaluate** — click **Evaluate my answer** to get it scored (see the next section).

> [!NOTE]
> Aim for a delivery of about **60–90 seconds**, a pace around **130–150 words per minute**, and as **few filler words** as possible. These are the same targets a real panel notices.

> [!WARNING]
> Speaking out loud takes a microphone and a browser that supports it (Chrome or Safari on a computer work best). If recording isn't available, you can always **type** your answer instead — everything else works exactly the same.

## 5. Getting your answer evaluated

When you click **Evaluate my answer**, the tool scores your response on the five things a PMM interviewer weighs:

- GTM Thinking and Strategy
- Value Proposition and Messaging
- Metric Selection and Commercial Ownership
- Cross-Functional Leadership
- Delivery, Pacing and Presence

Each is scored **1 to 5**, and the tool combines them into an **overall score out of 100**. Depending on how your coach set up the tool, evaluation happens one of two ways.

### Option A — Instant feedback (built-in AI)

If the built-in coach is turned on, you'll get your scores and written feedback right away — what worked, your top improvements, and one "power move" to elevate the answer. Your score is saved to the dashboard automatically.

### Option B — Copy-and-paste (no built-in AI)

If the built-in coach isn't available, a **Log your evaluation** panel appears. It's quick:

1. **Copy** — click into the prompt box and copy the whole prompt.
2. **Paste** — paste it into Claude or ChatGPT and send it.
3. **Read** — the AI replies with a clean, formatted evaluation: a score on each line, plus *What Worked*, *Improvements*, and a *Power Move*.
4. **Log** — read the five 1–5 scores off its reply, choose them in the dropdowns, and click **Save to dashboard**.

> [!TIP]
> Be honest when you log your scores. The dashboard is only useful if it reflects how you actually did — it's a mirror, not a scoreboard for anyone else.

## 6. Your Readiness Dashboard

The dashboard turns your practice into a picture of where you stand. Open it from the navigation menu.

- **Overall readiness** — the average of every answer you've evaluated.
- **Domain mastery** — a bar for each of the eight areas, so you can see your strengths and the gaps to work on next.
- **Readiness tiers** — as your average climbs, your standing moves from *Getting Started* to *Developing*, *Competitive*, *Interview Ready*, and *Offer Ready*.

> [!NOTE]
> Your scores come from real evaluations of your spoken answers — not from keywords or guesswork. A domain only shows a score once you've evaluated at least one question in it, so the picture fills in as you practice.

Use the dashboard to steer your prep: pick the lowest bars, practice a few questions in those areas, and watch them rise.

## 7. Saving and moving your progress

Your progress saves **automatically**. Every time you evaluate an answer, the score is stored in your browser on that device — so you can close the tab, shut your laptop, and come back later with everything intact. There's no "save" button to remember.

### Backing up or switching devices

Because progress is stored in one browser on one device, it won't automatically follow you to a different computer, a different browser, or your phone. The dashboard has three buttons to handle that:

- **Export** — downloads a small backup file of your scores. Keep it somewhere safe.
- **Import** — loads a backup file back in, on any device. You can merge it with what's there or replace it.
- **Reset** — clears all your scores to start fresh.

> [!WARNING]
> Two things will erase progress on a device: **clearing your browser data**, and using **private / incognito mode** (which forgets everything when you close it). If either applies to you, use **Export** regularly to keep a backup.

## 8. Frameworks, Battlecard, and Red Flags

Three reference areas round out your prep:

- **Frameworks** — ten PMM methods (positioning, GTM, pricing, growth, and more) with a one-line note on when to reach for each. Naming the right framework in an answer signals you operate with structure, not improvisation.
- **Battlecard** — a rapid-fire review sheet: concepts to know cold, metrics, power phrases, and common mistakes. Run through it the night before an interview.
- **Red Flags** — the ten anti-patterns that most often sink candidates, so you know exactly what to avoid.

## 9. Tips to get the most out of it

- **Say it, don't read it.** Practice out loud, not in your head. The gap between knowing an answer and saying it cleanly is where interviews are won or lost.
- **Work your weak spots.** Start with the areas where your dashboard bars are lowest, not the ones you already enjoy.
- **Build, don't memorize.** Don't memorize the scenario answers word-for-word. Learn the structure, then make it yours — interviewers can tell a recited script from real thinking.
- **Use your own stories.** For behavioral questions, the model answers are templates — swap in your own real stories and numbers. A genuine example beats a polished hypothetical every time.
- **Re-do your answers.** Evaluating the same question a second time, after working on the feedback, is one of the fastest ways to improve — and you'll see the score move.
- **Short and steady wins.** A few questions a day, evaluated honestly, beats cramming the night before.

## 10. Good to know and troubleshooting

**Will my progress be saved?**
Yes — automatically, in that browser on that device. Use **Export** to back it up or move it elsewhere.

**The microphone won't record.**
Use a computer with Chrome or Safari, allow microphone access when asked, or simply type your answer instead.

**I don't see instant AI feedback.**
That's expected on some setups. The copy-and-paste evaluation (Option B in Section 5) does the same job — just paste the prompt into Claude or ChatGPT.

**Is my practice private?**
Your practice answers and scores stay on your own device in your browser. They aren't sent anywhere or shared, unless you choose to **Export** and share the file.

**A question shows no full answer.**
Open the question and look for the **Generate a full model answer** button under *How to approach it*. Scenario questions use that instead of a fixed answer.
